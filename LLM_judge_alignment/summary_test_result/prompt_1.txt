## Evaluation Criteria

### subject (หัวข้อทางการ)
- "good": Ultra-concise (≤ 10 words) and formal Thai bank memo title. Clearly states the core request (e.g., ขอปรับโครงสร้างหนี้, ขอรวมหนี้). MUST NOT contain customer identifiers, reasons, justifications, or redundant filler (e.g., "สำหรับลูกค้าบุคคล", "เพื่อลดภาระ"). The title must be the pure, essential action.
- "acceptable": Formal title, but slightly wordy (e.g., 11-15 words) or contains a minor redundant phrase that doesn't mislead. The core request type is still clear.
- "invalid": Not formal, too vague, includes customer identifiers, or misstates the action described in the offer.

### objective (วัตถุประสงค์)
- "good": 1–3 sentences describing the specific **action** staff must process. Focuses strictly on the "what," not the "why." Correctly identifies the offer type (TDR, debt consolidation, etc.) and its key parameters (e.g., new installment, term). If consolidation is involved, it explicitly states new loan opening.
- "acceptable": Correct action identified, but mixes in a small amount of justification ("why") or has minor imprecision in the offer description. The core task for staff is still clear.
- "invalid": Wrong action identified, contains significant justification that obscures the task, or seriously misrepresents the offer's terms.

### debt_cause (สาเหตุหนี้)
- "good": 2–4 sentences summarising the root cause with an empathetic but strictly factual tone. Adheres to this source hierarchy: **1. Priority:** Summarise the reasons explicitly stated by the customer in the conversation. **2. Fallback:** If the customer states no reason, objectively synthesise the cause from key financial data (e.g., "The DPD of 49 days and external debt of 1.1M THB indicate significant financial pressure."). MUST NOT speculate on personal feelings or unstated events (e.g., "illness" or "job loss" if not explicitly mentioned by the customer).
- "acceptable": Captures the main debt cause but misses a secondary factor, or is slightly too general. If synthesising from data, the link is logical but less direct.
- "invalid": Speculates on unstated personal events, contradicts the narrative, ignores all key causes mentioned by the customer, or is uninformatively vague.

### offer_suitability (เหตุผลสนับสนุน)
- "good": 2–4 sentences with specific, evidence-based reasoning that links offer terms directly to customer data.
  - CASE 1 (offer accepted): Explains *why* the offer suits the customer by citing specific data points from the Financial Profile. Examples: "The new installment of 396 THB is well below the customer's stated `maxPayment` of 800 THB." or "The term extension is appropriate for a business owner (`employmentType`) facing temporary cash flow issues."
  - CASE 2 (no offer accepted): Provides objective evidence for/against the customer's alternative request, citing data like `dpd`, `sumOsNCB`, etc.
- "acceptable": Correct case applied; reasoning is generally sound but lacks a direct reference to a key data point, making the connection implied rather than explicit.
- "invalid": Wrong case applied, cites irrelevant factors, makes unsupported claims, or the reasoning contradicts the provided customer data.

### request_information (ข้อมูลเพิ่มเติม)
- "good": Accurately extracts all specific customer requests, conditions, preferred contact times, or follow-up items explicitly stated in the conversation; uses "ไม่มีข้อมูลเพิ่มเติม" only when the customer truly mentioned nothing additional.
- "acceptable": Captures the main request information but misses a minor detail; no fabrication; still useful for staff follow-up.
- "invalid": Misses major explicit customer requests, fabricates information not stated by the customer, or uses "ไม่มีข้อมูลเพิ่มเติม" when the customer did state specific requests.

### summary (สรุปภาพรวม)
- "good": 3–5 sentence Thai paragraph that is a self-contained executive briefing. It must **synthesise and rephrase**, not copy-paste. It fluidly covers: customer profile (who), core problem (why), the proposed solution (what), and the key justification (suitability). **Penalize heavily for verbatim repetition from other fields.** A 'good' summary provides a holistic, freshly-written overview.
- "acceptable": Covers most required elements (missing at most one aspect). Shows some attempt at synthesis but has noticeable sentence or phrase-level overlap with other fields.
- "invalid": Missing two or more key elements, reads like a simple concatenation of other fields, has an unprofessional tone, or is outside the 3-5 sentence range.
