## Evaluation Criteria

### subject (หัวข้อทางการ)
- "good": Highly concise (≤ 12 words) and formal Thai bank memo title. States the core request (e.g., ขอปรับโครงสร้างหนี้). May include a concise goal-oriented qualifier (e.g., "เพื่อรวมหนี้", "เพื่อลดค่างวด").
- "acceptable": Formal and clear, but slightly wordy (13-18 words) or contains a generic, non-essential qualifier about customer type (e.g., "สำหรับลูกค้ารายย่อย", "สำหรับลูกค้าบุคคล"). The core request is still clear.
- "invalid": Not formal, too long (>18 words), too vague, includes customer identifiers, or misstates the core action.

### objective (วัตถุประสงค์)
- "good": A direct, action-oriented statement (1-3 sentences) describing the specific task for staff. MUST clearly state the offer type (e.g., TDR) and all key parameters (e.g., accounts involved, new installment, new term). A brief subordinate clause for justification (e.g., "เพื่อลดภาระ...") or a confirmation of customer acceptance is acceptable and does not downgrade the rating. Avoids overly wordy intros like "วัตถุประสงค์ของคำขอนี้คือ...".
- "acceptable": The core action for staff is clear, but the text is slightly flawed. It might start with a wordy, unnecessary phrase (e.g., "วัตถุประสงค์คือ..."), have a justification that is too long/prominent, or miss a minor parameter (while still including the most critical ones).
- "invalid": Wrong action identified, missing multiple key parameters, or the justification completely obscures the core task.

### debt_cause (สาเหตุหนี้)
- "good": 2–4 sentences factually summarising the root cause. Follows source hierarchy: **1. Priority:** Accurately use reasons explicitly stated by the customer. **2. Fallback (Synthesis):** If no reason is stated, synthesise the cause by forming a **strong, explicit causal link** between financial data and the problem. It is not enough to list data points; the text must explain *how* one leads to the other. Example of a strong link: "The customer's high external debt of {sumOsNCB} THB creates a significant monthly burden, leading to a cash flow shortage that directly caused the {dpd}-day delinquency." It MUST NOT speculate on unstated personal events (e.g., "illness").
- "acceptable": Captures the main debt cause, but the logical link is weak, implied, or missing. For example, it lists data points (`sumOsNCB` is X, `dpd` is Y) without explaining their connection, or it correctly identifies the cause but fails to cite supporting data.
- "invalid": Speculates on unstated personal events, contradicts the narrative, ignores customer-stated causes, or is uninformatively vague (e.g., "customer has financial problems").

### offer_suitability (เหตุผลสนับสนุน)
- "good": 2–4 sentences of sharp, evidence-based reasoning. It must **explicitly connect the offer's key benefits to the core problem identified in the `debt_cause` field.** The reasoning must be supported by citing critical data points. Example: "The new installment of 396 THB directly addresses the affordability crisis by being well below the customer's stated `maxPayment` of 800 THB, resolving the delinquency issue." **Note:** If `debt_cause` is factually wrong/invalid, this field can still be "good" if its reasoning is sound and strongly supported by financial data (e.g., `{maxPayment}`, `{dpd}`).
- "acceptable": The reasoning is generally correct but less impactful. It might cite less critical data, make a generic claim (e.g., "helps the customer manage finances better"), or the link back to the core `debt_cause` is implied rather than explicit.
- "invalid": The reasoning is flawed, cites irrelevant factors, makes unsupported claims, or contradicts the provided customer data.

### request_information (ข้อมูลเพิ่มเติม)
- "good": Accurately extracts all specific customer requests, conditions, preferred contact times, or follow-up items explicitly stated in the conversation; uses "ไม่มีข้อมูลเพิ่มเติม" only when the customer truly mentioned nothing additional.
- "acceptable": Captures the main request information but misses a minor detail; no fabrication; still useful for staff follow-up.
- "invalid": Misses major explicit customer requests, fabricates information not stated by the customer, or uses "ไม่มีข้อมูลเพิ่มเติม" when the customer did state specific requests.

### summary (สรุปภาพรวม)
- "good": A 3–5 sentence Thai paragraph that serves as a self-contained executive briefing. It MUST be a **cohesive narrative that demonstrates deep understanding by logically connecting the key elements** (who, why, what, suitability) using transitional phrases to show cause and effect. It must read like a single, flowing thought, not a checklist. Minimal, incidental reuse of phrases from other fields is acceptable if the overall structure is a true synthesis.
- "acceptable": Covers the required elements (who, why, what, suitability), but the synthesis is weak. It reads like a disconnected list of facts or a simple concatenation of other fields (e.g., "The customer is X. Their problem is Y. The offer is Z. This is suitable because..."). Shows noticeable sentence-level reuse from other fields.
- "invalid": Missing two or more key elements, is a direct copy-paste or simple concatenation of other fields, has an unprofessional tone, or is outside the 3-5 sentence range.
